filament

Filament sends files directly between two devices — phone to phone, Android to iPhone, laptop to phone — from a normal browser tab. There is no app to install, no account to create, and your files are never uploaded to a server: they travel peer-to-peer over an encrypted WebRTC connection.

How it works

Devices on the same WiFi find each other automatically — like AirDrop, but it works across every platform. Devices on different networks pair with a one-time spoken code (say clever-lynx-63 across the table) or a shared link. The file then streams directly between the two browsers; a tiny signaling server only performs the introduction. A route badge shows you exactly how the bytes travel: LAN straight across your WiFi, P2P direct over the internet, or RELAY through our relay when networks are strict — encrypted in every case.

Why people use it

  • AirDrop alternative for Android ↔ iPhone — no ecosystem walls.
  • No size limit, no compression — send the original video, not the WhatsApp-crushed copy.
  • Private by architecture — there is no server copy to leak, expire, or be subpoenaed.
  • Resumes interrupted transfers — a dropped connection pauses and continues, instead of starting over.
  • Open source & self-hostable — run your own instance: github.com/Abdk4Moura/filament.

Compared to the alternatives

WeTransfer / email / Drive upload your file to someone else's storage first. WhatsApp recompresses media. AirDrop / Quick Share only work inside one vendor's ecosystem. Snapdrop-style sites inspired Filament — we added cross-network pairing codes, transfer resume, visible routing, and a self-hostable relay.
